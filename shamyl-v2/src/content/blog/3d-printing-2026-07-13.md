Here's your Thursday 3D printing roundup for July 13, 2026:

## Printer News & Releases

- **Kentstrapper Releases Mille System**: Italian manufacturer Kentstrapper has launched the Mille System, a massive 1 cubic meter build volume material extrusion printer priced at €40,000. This brings industrial-scale FDM printing to a more accessible price point.

- **Prusa Summer Flash Deal**: Original Prusa is running their Flash Summer Deal 2026 with 20% off the MK4S and reduced shipping on the CORE One+. Perfect timing if you've been considering upgrading your workshop.

- **Beehive Industries Orders NXG 600E**: Beehive Industries is betting big on ultra-large-format metal additive manufacturing with a multi-unit order of NXG 600E systems. The demand for large-scale metal AM continues to grow.

- **Prusa INDX July Update**: The Prusa INDX Founder's Edition is shipping with updates on what's next for the platform. The modular tool-changing system is gaining traction among serious makers.

## Materials & Filaments

- **Elegoo x Emoji® Collaboration**: Elegoo has partnered with Emoji® to introduce more people to 3D printing. This fun collaboration aims to make 3D printing more approachable for beginners through familiar characters and designs.

- **Prusament PLA High Speed**: Prusa released a new Prusament PLA optimized for high-speed printing. Perfect for rapid prototyping when you need quality prints fast.

- **Prusament Resin Model+**: A new generation resin with safer health profile and better impact resistance. The ongoing push for safer resin formulations continues.

- **EOS Partners with Constellium**: EOS is expanding their aluminum portfolio for additive manufacturing through a partnership with Constellium, bringing more aerospace-grade aluminum options to the AM market.

## Software & Updates

- **Open Community License v1.1**: Prusa has released the new Open Community License with an improved plugin system, more examples, and addressed community questions. The ecosystem around PrusaSlicer continues to mature.

- **ColorMix for EasyPrint and PrusaSlicer**: A new open-source ColorMix feature allows printing with dozens of colors. Multi-material printing without the hardware complexity of MMU or Palette systems.

- **Tripo AI Raises $150M**: Tripo AI secured a massive $150M Series A3 round, showing continued investor confidence in AI-powered 3D generation and modeling tools.

## Cool Projects & Prints

- **Zaha Hadid Architects' 6-Meter Tower**: The renowned architecture firm has 3D printed a six-meter tower for Terminal Expo using large-scale additive manufacturing. Architectural concrete printing continues to push boundaries.

- **3D Printed Macropad for Smart Home**: A custom 3D-printed macropad project for controlling smart home devices shows how functional prints can integrate into daily life.

- **3D-Printed Stencil Airbrushing**: Simple 3D-printed stencil tips and tricks are making airbrushing more versatile and accessible for hobbyists and professionals alike.

- **Fake Studio Lighting with Filament Mixing**: A clever technique using filament mixing and a free app to create fake studio lighting effects on 3D prints. The community never stops innovating!

## Industry Applications

- **Venus Aerospace Closes $91M**: Venus Aerospace raised $91M to scale detonation engine production, likely leveraging AM for complex engine components that are impossible to manufacture traditionally.

- **Protolabs Adds New Resin**: Protolabs added a new resin to their Hi-Speed SLA lineup on Axtra3D's Lumia X1, expanding their rapid prototyping capabilities.

- **Nigeria's New STEAM Centre**: Nigeria is betting on hands-on 3D printing skills with a new Czech-backed STEAM Centre. Global AM education initiatives continue to expand.

- **RIMPAC 2026 Military Applications**: Military applications of 3D printing were highlighted at RIMPAC 2026, showing how armed forces worldwide are adopting additive for field repair and rapid manufacturing.

## Kickstarter & Campaigns

- **Bambu Lab Earthquake Relief**: Bambu Lab is turning filament sales into earthquake relief for Venezuela, showing how the maker community can drive positive social impact.

- **XTPL Adds Japanese Customer**: 3D printed packaging OEM XTPL has added another Japanese customer, showing growing Asian market adoption of micro-precision AM technologies.

- **Formnext Asia Shenzhen**: New Maker Day and dealer matching events at Formnext Asia Shenzhen are connecting manufacturers with regional partners.

### Key Takeaway

The 3D printing industry continues its rapid evolution with massive funding rounds ($150M for Tripo AI, $91M for Venus Aerospace) signaling strong investor confidence. Meanwhile, practical innovations like Prusa's ColorMix and high-speed filaments make advanced techniques more accessible to everyday makers. The line between industrial and desktop additive manufacturing keeps blurring—whether you're running a NXG 600E for metal parts or a MK4S in your garage, the tools for creation are more capable than ever.
