Daily AI Updates - May 13, 2026

#daily-digest#AI#tech-news#market-updates#startups#technology-trends#artificial-intelligence

Here’s your daily roundup of AI and tech news for May 13, 2026:

AI Developments

  • Google Puts Gemini at the Center of Android: Google is rolling out new Gemini-powered Android features ahead of I/O, with AI that understands screen context and completes multi-step tasks like building shopping carts or booking reservations. The move comes ahead of Apple’s expected Siri revamp.

  • Meta AI Gets “Live AI” Feature: Meta’s AI app now supports real-time camera interaction — point your camera at something, ask about it, and get instant responses. This brings Meta’s multimodal capabilities into everyday use cases.

  • OpenAI Brings GPT-5.5-Class Reasoning to Real-Time Voice: OpenAI has enhanced voice mode with advanced reasoning capabilities, enabling voice agents to orchestrate complex multi-step tasks in real-time conversations.

  • Microsoft’s AI Scanner Finds Critical Windows Vulnerabilities: Microsoft’s MDASH AI vulnerability scanner discovered four critical remote code execution (RCE) bugs in Windows TCP/IP networking, demonstrating AI’s growing role in cybersecurity.

  • SAP Unveils the Autonomous Enterprise: SAP announced a unified Business AI Platform at Sapphire, deepening partnerships with Anthropic, AWS, Google Cloud, Microsoft, NVIDIA, and Palantir for enterprise automation.

Technology Trends

  • Agentic AI Goes Mainstream: Google’s new Android features showcase “vibe-coded widgets” and agentic AI that can take actions on behalf of users, signaling a shift from passive assistants to active agents.

  • Meta Llama 4 Solidifies Open-Source Leadership: With native multimodal vision capabilities released earlier this month, Meta’s Llama 4 continues to close the gap with proprietary models while remaining open-source.

  • NVIDIA Dynamo 1.0 Enters Production: NVIDIA’s inference operating system for AI factories is now production-ready, optimizing how AI models run at scale in data centers.

Market Updates

  • Anthropic in Talks for $30B Raise at $900B Valuation: Anthropic is reportedly negotiating a massive funding round that would value the company at $900 billion, highlighting continued investor appetite for foundation model companies.

  • NVIDIA Stock Hits Record $219.44: NVIDIA shares surged to an all-time closing high as AI demand continues to power gains ahead of May 20 earnings. The company has committed over $40 billion this year to AI supply chain investments.

  • SAP Acquires Dremio for Data Lakehouse: SAP’s acquisition of Dremio aims to unify SAP and non-SAP data to power agentic AI applications in the enterprise.

  • Cisco Acquires Astrix Security for $400M: Cisco completed its acquisition of Israeli AI security startup Astrix Security, strengthening its cybersecurity portfolio.

World Tech

  • France Orders 2.5M Civil Servants to Switch to Linux: In a major digital sovereignty move, France has ordered its civil servants to ditch Microsoft Windows for Linux, reducing dependence on US tech giants.

  • Google and SpaceX in Talks for Orbital Data Centers: Reports indicate Google and SpaceX are negotiating to launch data centers into orbit, potentially revolutionizing edge computing and data sovereignty.

Key Takeaway

The AI race is accelerating on multiple fronts: Google is embedding Gemini deeply into Android before Apple’s AI reboot, Meta is making AI multimodal and real-time, and enterprises like SAP are betting big on agentic automation. Meanwhile, the market continues to reward AI infrastructure players — NVIDIA’s record stock price and Anthropic’s potential $900B valuation show investors remain bullish despite regulatory concerns.
