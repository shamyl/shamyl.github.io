Pakistan Tech Roundup - July 13, 2026

#pakistan-tech#pakistan#startups#IT-exports#telecom#ecosystem

Here’s your Saturday Pakistan tech roundup for July 13, 2026:

IT Exports & Economy

Pakistan’s IT export sector continues its remarkable momentum, achieving a historic milestone:

  • Tech exports hit record $4.2 billion as current account returns to surplus — marking a significant achievement for Pakistan’s digital economy (The Express Tribune, June 17)
  • IT exports rose 13% to $373 million in May, taking the 11-month total to $4.2 billion (Profit by Pakistan Today, June 17)
  • Services exports surged to $8.27 billion, led by IT sector growth (Dawn, June 7)
  • IT exports grew nearly 20% to $2.61 billion in the first seven months of FY2026 (Business Recorder, Feb 18)
  • Freelancer exports crossed $950 million, showcasing the strength of Pakistan’s independent digital workforce (The Express Tribune, May 26)

Telecom & Connectivity

Major developments in Pakistan’s telecommunications landscape as the country prepares for 5G:

  • Telenor Pakistan and Ufone formally merged after Islamabad High Court approval — a historic consolidation creating a stronger telecom operator (Dawn, July 1)
  • IT Minister emphasized the proposed telecom bill is essential as existing laws are insufficient for 5G and modern technologies (Dawn, July 5)
  • National Assembly passed the telecom amendment bill, paving the way for improved digital infrastructure (The Nation, June 11)
  • Jazz announced 5G rollout will expand to 1,000 sites by mid-August (Arab News, May 7)
  • Right-of-way reform identified as critical for digital economy growth (The Express Tribune, June 21)
  • Government clarified that revised telecom bill makes landowner consent mandatory for right-of-way, addressing privacy concerns (Profit by Pakistan Today, July 6)

Startups & Funding

Pakistan’s startup ecosystem shows resilience with new developments:

  • Pakistan’s AI ecosystem expanding in 2026 — new startups and software solutions emerging across the country (Digital Pakistan, June 16)
  • Uplift AI raised $3.5 million in seed funding, aiming to create thousands of jobs in Pakistan (Business Recorder, Jan 29)
  • Pakistan startups secured over $74 million in funding during 2025, according to industry reports (Business Recorder, Jan 15)
  • Airlift co-founder’s new startup Metal secured strategic investment (Business Recorder, June 2)
  • Pakistani founders making global strides in artificial intelligence, contributing to international AI developments (The Nation, Nov 4, 2025)
  • AI Seekho launched with Rs2.5 million prize pool to promote AI education and innovation (The Express Tribune, April 16)

E-commerce & FinTech

The financial technology sector continues to evolve:

  • Fintech Landscape of Pakistan in 2026 — comprehensive analysis shows rapid adoption of digital payment solutions (The Fintech Times, July 10)
  • Pakistan’s economy set to expand with fintech adoption, though regulatory clarity remains a key priority (Crowdfund Insider, June 7)
  • Mobile wallets and digital bank accounts without biometric verification face blocking as SBP tightens regulations (ProPakistani, Oct 24, 2025)

Government Initiatives

Policy developments supporting the tech ecosystem:

  • Finance Minister presented Economic Survey 2025-26, highlighting digital economy growth as IT sector posts gains (Dawn, June 12)
  • Pakistan economy grew 3.7% in FY26 — fastest in four years, driven partly by IT sector performance (Asia News Network, June 12)
  • IT exports and record remittances strengthened Pakistan’s external resilience (Independent News Pakistan, July 6)
  • Government eyes major tax relief for telecom sector in FY27 budget to accelerate digital transformation (Business Recorder, May 18)

Events & Community

Upcoming opportunities and industry gatherings:

  • Pakistan’s militancy-hit Balochistan to host its first trade and investment expo this month, showcasing regional tech potential (Arab News, July 1)
  • inDrive announced Aurora Tech Awards 2026 for Pakistani women startup founders (Biz Today, Aug 22, 2025)
  • Pakistan to host first international gemstone exhibition in July to unlock untapped export potential (Arab News, April 27)

Key Takeaway

Pakistan’s tech ecosystem is experiencing a pivotal moment. The historic $4.2 billion IT export milestone demonstrates the sector’s growing maturity and global competitiveness. Simultaneously, the Telenor-Ufone merger and impending 5G rollout signal a transformation in digital infrastructure. While the startup ecosystem navigates funding challenges, AI-focused ventures and strategic investments show promise. The key to sustaining this momentum will be regulatory clarity, infrastructure investment, and continued support for digital skills development.
